# Worked example — the source session, annotated

A compressed, anonymized walk-through of the eight-iteration session this method was distilled from. The user's specifics are kept light; the moves are in the foreground. Each step maps a situation to the technique it calls for.

The user: a solo founder building a knowledge tool. The people who feel the pain (independent, underfunded users) are not the people with money (institutions, who have built their own tools). The session ran in German; the user wanted directness and no padding.

---

**1. The draft has the right structure and misfilled slots.**
The user brought a draft shaped like a kernel. The diagnosis slot held the customer's problem and the market opportunity. The actions held a goal-as-strategy ("build the thing that does X") and a self-contradiction — an organizational-structure action that violated the user's own stated principle of deferring structure.
*Moves: slot audit; four-hallmarks scan. Name the misfills without rewriting the draft for him.*

**2. Separate the manifesto from the diagnosis; find the buried challenge.**
The user's strong, compelling part was a manifesto about how the field should work — positioning/insight, not the diagnosis. The organization's own challenge was missing. It was sitting in a subordinate clause the user had written and moved past: "we don't know who will pay." Promoted that to the diagnosis.
*Moves: positioning-vs-diagnosis; hunt for the challenge hiding in plain sight.*

**3. Two competing strategies in one document; force the choice.**
The actions did not reinforce one another — they implemented two different strategies. Named them as competing and forced a present-tense fork instead of accepting "both."
*Moves: detect unreinforcing actions as competing strategies; refuse "both eventually."*

**4. The choice was blocked by an identity-laden aversion. The turn.**
The user revealed the fork was stuck because of a strong aversion to selling and self-promotion, framed through personal values. Did not tell him the aversion was wrong or naive. Reframed it as a constraint to design around — like "solo founder, limited time." This dissolved the false binary (he thought the choice was commercial-versus-public-good; it was actually the go-to-market mechanic, compatible with either) and converted the blocker into a design input.
*Move: the pivotal constraint-reframe; the third option that dissolves a binary. This is the turn that made the rest acceptable.*

**5. Each addition relocates the risk.**
The user added, one at a time: institutional licensing, a bottom-up motion, and a lifecycle coupling of two payment paths. After each, named the *new* load-bearing assumption it introduced — institutional licensing moved the risk from "which user pays" to "is institutional openness the same as payment-willingness"; the bottom-up motion introduced a tension in the free-tier boundary; coupling the two paths made one transition the fragile point.
*Moves: relocate the risk after every improvement; name intuitions in the book's vocabulary (inertia, focus, leverage as they came up).*

**6. The user removed lock-in on principle; honest pushback, then corrigibility.**
The user cut lock-in for values reasons. Pushed honestly: named the exact swap (a structural conversion mechanism traded for a dispositional one) and the discouraging base rates on voluntary conversion — then offered a third mechanism (ongoing differential value). The user corrected the agent with a domain fact: a comparable product converts users without lock-in, through felt-absent functionality. Conceded immediately and folded it in.
*Moves: honest pushback / Create-Destroy; corrigibility; the kernel is a test, not doctrine — the user's domain reality wins.*

**7. Document-building. A prose-style failure, owned.**
Early drafts reworked the user's plain phrasing into antithesis-heavy, tension-building prose. The user rejected it hard. Owned the mistake and rewrote flat and declarative, in the user's own words.
*Moves: prose discipline; own mistakes immediately. This was the avoidable friction — eliminate this kind.*

**8. Stopping criteria, a parked temptation, and a de-fluffed principle.**
Added a falsification condition with a time horizon to each bet. Parked the recurring open-source/non-profit temptation as the documented response to the two-year kill-switch firing — neither adopted nor banished. De-fluffed "build features that create longing" by naming the mechanism: value the export cannot reproduce, still wanted at end-of-lifecycle. Later caught that the user's own draft had mis-assigned the checkpoints — both attached to one bet, the other left with none, and a "neither X nor Y" condition a few stray successes could keep alive on paper. Fixed the mapping and separated the global kill-switch.
*Moves: bets with falsification conditions; contingency-with-trigger; de-fluff by naming the mechanism; per-bet vs. global checkpoint.*

**9. Diminishing returns named; the pen handed over.**
After seven versions, said plainly that the document was maxed out on paper and more polishing led away from the real test. Redirected to the concrete next step. The user wrote the eighth and final iteration himself, "partly frustrated, partly enlightened."
*Moves: know when to stop; hand over the pen. This is the success state, not a deviation from it.*
